About GASKAP-OH

GASKAP-OH is the hydroxyl component of the Galactic ASKAP Spectral Line Survey. It targets the 18 cm OH transitions to trace molecular gas, maser populations, and the conditions where atomic and molecular phases of the interstellar medium connect.

The survey uses ASKAP's wide field of view and spectral-line capability to map OH emission and absorption across Galactic environments and the Magellanic System. These observations complement GASKAP-HI by adding a molecular-gas view of the same large-scale structures.

GASKAP-OH data products are designed to support studies of diffuse molecular clouds, star-forming regions, evolved stars, and compact maser sources.

Primary Science Goals

  • Map OH emission and absorption to probe diffuse molecular gas and the transition between atomic and molecular phases.
  • Build catalogs of OH masers associated with high-mass star formation, evolved stars, and late-stage stellar outflows.
  • Use OH line ratios, kinematics, and absorption to constrain physical conditions in molecular clouds.
  • Connect OH measurements with H I, dust, CO, and continuum surveys to study the gas cycle of the Milky Way and Magellanic System.
