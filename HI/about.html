About GASKAP

The Galactic ASKAP Survey (GASKAP) is one of several approved priority survey science projects to be undertaken with the Australian Square Kilometre Array Pathfinder (ASKAP), a 36-element interferometer located in Western Australia that uses innovative phased array feed technology to maximize sensitivity and field of view.

ASKAP's long baselines, extending to approximately 6 km, give synthesized beams at resolutions between 20 and 30 arcseconds within a 30 square degree field of view. Coupled with narrow spectral resolutions down to approximately 0.2 km s-1, GASKAP will provide an unprecedented view of the neutral gas content of the Milky Way and nearby Magellanic System.

Over approximately 8000 allocated hours, GASKAP will map up to +/-10 degrees in Galactic latitude over all Galactic longitudes, in addition to the two Magellanic Clouds, Bridge, and Stream.

Primary Science Goals

  • Construct a comprehensive picture of the multi-phase nature of the interstellar medium in the Milky Way and Magellanic System through observations of neutral hydrogen at 21 cm and the three 18 cm OH lines.
  • Study the gas cycle of the interstellar medium, including how gas flows into and out of the Milky Way.
  • Use GASKAP's spectral and angular resolution to study stellar feedback, energy flow into the interstellar medium, diffuse molecular clouds through OH emission, and cold gas properties through absorption.
  • Discover and catalog thousands of OH masers as tracers of stellar evolution, high-mass star formation, and late-stage outflows from red supergiants.

Pilot observations were completed in 2022, with the full survey planned to begin in 2023.
